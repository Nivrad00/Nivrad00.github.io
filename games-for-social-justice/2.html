Race and culture

Video games, a historically white-dominated field, have made great strides in representing diverse races and cultures. However, games can do more than passively reflect. Games made by developers of color, or in collaboration with marginalized racial groups, can amplify previously untold stories. And by allowing players to experience the struggles of someone of another race, they can foster empathy and cross-cultural understanding.

"Walk a mile in my shoes": A virtual world exercise for fostering students' subjective understandings of the experiences of people of color

John Tawa

This article demonstrates the potential of using immersion in virtual worlds to educate players about race. It describes an academic project in which students controlled avatars of a different race from their own in the virtual world game Second Life.

How Miles Morales in his own Spider-Man video game confronts racial disparity in gaming

Nicole Chavez

Authentic depictions of people of color in non-serious games can also play a part in social change. This article discusses the development and impact of Marvel's Spider-Man: Miles Morales, which features an Afro-Latin protagonist, in the context of a lack of racial diversity in commercial games.

Games

Real Lives 2010

Educational Simulations

This extensive simulation game allows players to experience life as a random human from any country in the world, and incorporates tons of real life statistics.This game definitely falls on the "edutainment" end of the spectrum, but will likely widen players' perspective in one way or another.

Visit the website

Everyday Racism

University of Western Sydney, Deakin University, Melbourne University

A mobile app that puts the player in the shoes of someone who experiences racism for one real-life week. Through simulated emails, social media, and more, this app aims to both increase empathy and give players practice responding to microaggressions.

Get the app for free

Falcon Age

Outerloop Games

Play as a falcon tamer rebelling against robotic colonizers that have destroyed your planet's culture and environment. This game boasts sci-fi action adventure gameplay and an adorable virtual pet, but also has a strong anti-colonialist and anti-capitalist narrative. Also available in VR!

For a great breakdown of this game's social message, see this article by Dante Douglas.

Visit the website

Hair Nah

Momo Pixel

A short, tongue-in-cheek game about a Black woman fending off intruding hands that want to touch her hair. A great way to start conversations on microaggressions... or just commiserate.

Play online for free

Parable of the Polygons

Vi Hart, Nicky Case

An interactive blog post by Vi Hart and Nicky Case that demonstrates the importance of actively seeking diversity. This message is delivered in a profound, yet accessible way by letting the reader explore a simple simulation involving communities of triangles and squares.

Read online for free

Never Alone

Upper One Games

This gorgeous puzzle-platformer game was made in partnership with storytellers and elders of the Iñupiat tribe in Alaska. By drawing on traditional stories and art, it provides a glimpse into the folklore of a people too often excluded from the cultural consciousness of North America.
