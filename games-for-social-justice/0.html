What do games have to do with social justice?

  • Games are popular – 67% of Americans play them
  • Games can introduce players to experiences other than their own
  • Games can be deeply transformative

Video games are often written off as mindless fun. But like any other form of art – books, movies, or music – games can have purposes other than entertainment. These so-called serious games have been applied to countless fields, from language learning to physical therapy. But can they play a part in social justice?

In short, games put players in other people's shoes. Through non-normative mechanics, narratives, and characters, games can provide a voice for those on the margins, and foster awareness and empathy in others. As a form of media with a broad appeal and a potential for deep immersion, video games are perfectly suited to nurture a more compassionate world.

What's in this guide?

This annotated bibliography is intended as a brief exploration of the breadth and power of video games that further social justice. It is focused on American audiences and is organized by social issue, with each section containing:

  • Recommended serious and non-serious games that address social justice
  • Popular and academic articles discussing the impact of such games

Who's it for?

  • New researchers surveying the field of serious games
  • Librarians and educators curating games or using them in lessons and programming
  • Individuals looking for game recommendations

What's not covered?

The guide focuses on games with social justice as a primary purpose. Many highly important issues have been excluded due to scope constraints, including:

  • Women in games
  • Games and the military industrial complex
  • Games and the environment
  • Game accessibility
  • Game design and development
  • Issues in game communities and the game development industry
