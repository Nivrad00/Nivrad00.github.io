Overview

This page contains resources not limited to a particular social issue. In particular, they aim to answer this question: How do games bridge the gap from entertainment to social change?

Articles

An overview of serious games

Fedwa Laamarti, Mohamad Eid, and Abdulmotaleb El Saddik

Oriented toward researchers, this survey categorizes serious games by application area and provides numerous examples of each. It also briefly discusses game design and research in the field. A good framework for understanding where games for social justice fit in.

Read online

Laamarti, F., Eid, M., & el Saddik, A. (2014). An overview of serious games. International Journal of Computer Games Technology, 2014. https://doi.org/10.1155/2014/358152

For a better world: Digital games and the social change sector

Benjamin Stokes, Suzanne Seggerman, and David Rejeski

Demonstrates how the unique benefits of games align with the needs of social change organizations. Also provides concrete strategies for integrating said games. Though targeted to nonprofits, this is a good resource for anyone who needs to convince stakeholders of the social value of games.

Read online

Stokes, B., Seggerman, S., Rejeski, D., & Wei, H. (n.d.). For a better world: Digital games and the social change sector. Games For Change. https://www.gamesforchange.org/g4cwp/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/g4cwhitepaper.pdf

Game changers: Playing games for good

Mary Flanagan (Video)

A talk by game designer and researcher Mary Flanagan, who identifies four principles on how games create social impact. For example, games can help address tough issues by fictionalizing them. Flanagan draws on her prior work as examples, sharing concrete results that demonstrate the effectiveness of such games.

More of Flanagan's work can be found at her lab Tiltfactor, but unfortunately the digital games have been deprecated by Flash as of the writing of this guide.

Watch on YouTube

Flanagan, M. [World Economic Forum]. (2018, January 25). Game changers: Playing games for good [Video]. YouTube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=28qu-RqCW4A

Designing games to foster empathy

Johnathan Belman and Mary Flanagan

Provides psychology-derived design principles for games that promote empathy. Though intended for game designers, this is a great science-backed argument that games can create empathy for marginalized groups, and can help with the selection and evaluation of games. Highlights games PeaceMaker, Hush, and Layoff to demonstrate these principles.

Read online (pages 11-21)

Belman, J., & Flanagan, M. (2010). Designing games to foster empathy. Cognitive Technology, 14(2), 11–21. https://www.academia.edu/243594/Special_Issue_of_Cognitive_Technology_on_Games_for_Good

Other Resources

Games for Change

A non-profit that hosts everything from annual game festivals to pop-up game installations. Their website contains a curated list of over 175 games that engage social issues. Also, the G4C Student Challenge program may be of interest to educators looking to bring serious games to their schools
