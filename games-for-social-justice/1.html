An Overview of Serious Games

A wide-reaching survey of the field with numerous examples.

Read online

Oriented toward researchers, this article categorizes serious games by application area and provides numerous examples of each. It also briefly discusses game design and research in the field. A good framework for understanding where games for social justice fit in.

Laamarti, F., Eid, M., & el Saddik, A. (2014). An overview of serious games. International Journal of Computer Games Technology, 2014. https://doi.org/10.1155/2014/358152

For a Better World: Digital Games and the Social Change Sector

Aligns the unique benefits of games with the needs of social change nonprofits.

Read online

Discusses the needs of social change organizations, the unique benefits that games can provide to them, and concrete strategies for integrating said games. Though targeted to nonprofits, this is a good resource for anyone who needs to convince stakeholders of the social value of games.

Stokes, B., Seggerman, S., Rejeski, D., & Wei, H. (n.d.). For a better world: Digital games and the social change sector. Games For Change. https://www.gamesforchange.org/g4cwp/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/g4cwhitepaper.pdf

Game Changers: Playing Games for Good (Video)

Concretely demonstrates the impact of research-based social change games.

Watch on YouTube

A talk by game designer and researcher Mary Flanagan, who identifies four principles on how games create social impact. For example, games can help address tough issues by fictionalizing them. Flanagan draws on her prior work as examples, sharing concrete results that demonstrate the effectiveness of such games.

More of Flanagan's work can be found at her lab Tiltfactor, but unfortunately the digital games have been deprecated by Flash as of the writing of this guide.

Flanagan, M. [World Economic Forum]. (2018, January 25). Game changers: Playing games for good [Video]. YouTube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=28qu-RqCW4A

Designing games to foster empathy

Provides psychology-derived design principles for games that promote empathy.

Read online (pages 11-21)

Though this article is most relevant to game designers, it's a great science-backed argument that games can create empathy for marginalized groups, and can be used as a guideline for selecting and evaluating games. It also explores games PeaceMaker, Hush, and Layoff to demonstrate these principles.

Belman, J., & Flanagan, M. (2010). Designing games to foster empathy. Cognitive Technology, 14(2), 11–21. https://www.academia.edu/243594/Special_Issue_of_Cognitive_Technology_on_Games_for_Good

Games for Change

Games, events, and educator resources for social impact.

https://www.gamesforchange.org

G4C is a non-profit that does everything from annual game festivals to pop-up game installations. Their website contains a curated list of over 175 games that engage social issues. Also, the G4C Student Challenge program may be of interest to educators looking to bring serious games to their schools
