Poverty, immigration, and war

War has long been a staple of video games. Yet, these games are usually told from the perspective of the powerful, whether it's a nation duking it out for territory or the U.S. military swooping in to stop terrorism. The games on this page attempt to tell the counterstory, of those who are economically and politically powerless, and thus put a human face on these often-debated issues.

Articles

Spent: Changing students' affective learning toward homelessness through persuasive video game play

Dana Ruggiero

A study that empirically demonstrates a change in students' attitudes toward homelessness after playing a social change game. This experiment found that playing the game Spent lead to higher affective learning – or, internalization of positive attitudes – than reading a first-person narrative or doing nothing.

Read online

Ruggiero, D. N. (2014). Spent: Changing students’ affective learning toward homelessness through persuasive video game play. Proceedings of the SIGCHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems. Published. https://doi.org/10.1145/2556288.2557390

Cultivating transcultural understanding through migration-related videogames

Esther Chin, Dan Golding

An academic overview of how video games about migration can foster understanding of cultural diversity. Analyzes three migration-related games: Escape from Woomera, Papers, Please, and Everyday Racism.

Read via UNC

Chin, E., & Golding, D. (2016). Cultivating transcultural understanding through migration-related videogames. Asia Pacific Media Educator, 26(1), 83–98. https://doi.org/10.1177/1326365X16640346

Games

Papers, Please

Lucas Pope

Decide the fate of immigrants as a border inspector working for a dystopian country. This award-winning game, famous for embodying games as an art form, pairs paper-checking puzzles with difficult moral decisions that demand empathy from the player.

Visit the website

This War of Mine

11 bit studios

In this gripping survival simulator, you control a group of civilians struggling to obtain food, stay healthy, and stave off depression in a war-torn land. By allowing players to feel the desperation and horror of war first-hand, this game puts a hauntingly human face on the subject and pushes back against the pro-war narrative of military shooters.

Visit the website

PeaceMaker

ImpactGames

A landmark serious game that challenges you to achieve peace between Israel and Palestine. Not only does this game provide a roughly accurate background on the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, it also demonstrates first-hand the complexity and unpredictability of global politics, and provides a glimpse of how peace can and should be achieved.

Spent

McKinney, Urban Ministries of Durham

This short web-based game forces you to make difficult decisions to survive a month on $1000. By demonstrating how changes in employment, housing, and other expenses can have a devastating impact on your life, it creates empathy for the homeless and impoverished and delivers the message that economic hardship can happen to anyone.
